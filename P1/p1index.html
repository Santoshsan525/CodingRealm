APJ Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

India's first space mission was launched by APJ on 26th October 1975. Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and politician. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to the fields of aerospace engineering and missile development in India.
Here are some key highlights of his life and career:
1. **Scientific Contributions:** Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was involved in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III. He also played a crucial role in the development of ballistic missile technology, particularly the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
2. **Presidency:** Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the "People's President" and was widely respected for his humility and approachability during his term.
3. **Wings of Fire:** He authored the book "Wings of Fire," which is an autobiography that narrates his early life, work in India's space and missile programs, and his vision for India's future.
4. **Abdul Kalam's Vision:** Dr. Kalam was an advocate for science, education, and self-reliance in India. He promoted technological advancements and believed that science and education could be powerful tools for national development.
5. **Awards and Honors:** Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. He was also recognized with honorary doctorates from various universities around the world.
6. **Youth Inspiration:** Dr. Abdul Kalam was known for his interactions with students and young people. He often spoke at educational institutions, inspiring the youth with his vision and encouraging them to pursue careers in science and technology.
7. **Legacy:** Dr. Kalam's legacy lives on, and he is remembered as a great scientist, teacher, and statesman. He continues to be an inspiration to people in India and beyond.
Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's life and work serve as a testament to the potential of individuals to bring about positive change through dedication, innovation, and a commitment to the progress of their nation.
